About

I am Zulfa Nur'aini 'Afifah, a student of MSc Cartography. My master's program is Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degree Program which is hosted by Technische Universität München. The partner universities of this program are Technische Universität Wien, Technische Universität Dresden, and ITC - University of Twente. I am also a graduate of BSc Cartography and Remote Sensing from Gadjah Mada University, Indonesia.

Cartography has always been my main interest! I found that the most exciting thing about cartography is the visualisation of geospatial data and the method of obtaining feedback from map users. Becoming an academia with this interest is challenging yet exciting for me. I like to conduct a research and be a student assistant! See my previous publications in my CV below😇✨

THEMPE

THEMatic Mapping Practice and Explanation

Thempe (/tempe/) is an acronym of Thematic Mapping Practice and Explanation. Inspired by Tempeh, a Javanese food which is written in Javanese as: ꦠꦺꦩ꧀ꦥꦺ (Tempeh in Wikipedia). By learning map types in Thempe you will gain more knowledge in the simplest and exciting way. Are you ready for more?

FIELD WORK

Pangandaran, West Java, Indonesia (2017)

Data Acquisition Project

Kuliah Kerja Lapangan 2 (KKL 2) or Field Work / Field School for Cartography and Remote Sensing program in Universitas Gadjah Mada. Our group consisted of 11 students and we worked on this project together to do data acquisition. The group data acquisition methods that we use are bathymetric measurements, residential block surveys, aerial photography, object recognition, terrestrial surveys, and accuracy tests. We carried out all these methods according to the regional segmentation in Pangandaran, West Java.

FIELD WORK

Padang, West Sumatera, Indonesia (2018)

Utilization of sentinel-2A imagery for mapping the dynamics of total suspended sediment at the river mouth of the Padang City

Kuliah Kerja Lapangan 3 (KKL 3) or Field Work / Field School for Cartography and Remote Sensing program in Universitas Gadjah Mada. Our group consisted of 6 students and we worked on this project together from data acquisition, data preprocessing, data collection from the field, and data analysis. At the end of this project, we visualize it into a multi-temporal map for the area around the coast of Padang, West Sumatra.

Community Service Program

Galo-Galo, Morotai, North Maluku, Indonesia (2018)

This project is a mandatory action that is carried out by all students of Universitas Gadjah Mada in their 6th semester. In my team, we consist of 29 students who come from different cities across Indonesia and have study backgrounds from different programs at Gadjah Mada University. We received financial and moral support from the university and government of Morotai, North Maluku to implement our program. We have a variety of programs aimed at improving the quality of life of the local community where we serve for 2 months. Even though the project is only 2 months, we have been preparing for about a year to prepare everything. Our biggest program is the Morotai Festival, where all Morotai residents participate in this festival to celebrate independence day and promote Morotai to the wider Indonesian community.
